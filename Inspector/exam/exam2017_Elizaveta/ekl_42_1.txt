The chart illustrates the difference in unemployement rate in five world areas. Figures are given for 2014 and 2015. 
Overall, the percentage of people without job remained the same in 2014 and 2015 in S. Asia and N. Africa. Moreover, S. Asia had the smallest rate of unemployement, whereas N. Africa had the highest. 
Looking at numbers that changed during the period showen, there are two regions that faced decrease in rate of jobless people. Middle East experienced the fall in numbers, from 11% by 1,4%, while Latin America's unemployment rate went down by 0,4% (from 6,8% to 5,8%). On the contrary, in EU the number of people, who don't have job increased negligibly to 7,9% in 2015 compared with 7,5% in 2014). 
As far as the regions where the rate remained the same it can be named N. Africa, where the rate was 12,5% both for 2014 and 2015, as well as the figure in S. Asia remained at 3,9%. Worldwide rate also showed stability with 5,9% of people being unemployed.
