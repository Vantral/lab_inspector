The bar chart gives information on how the level of unemployment changed in different regions of the world compared to the situation worldwide in 2014 and 2015. 
In general, North Africa and Middle East have the highest level of unemployment in comparison to EU, Latin America, South Asia and worldwide. 
To begin, the unemployment rate in North Africa remained the same in 2014 and 2015 and was the highest among the given world regions, accounting for 12,5% in both years. Middle East was the second world region which had comparatively high level of unemployment in 2014 and 2015, accounting for 11% and 9.6% respectively. These countries had a high unemployment rate compared to the situation worldwide. 
Next, the level of unemployment in other regions, namely, EU, Latin America and South Asia, was lower in the period given. For instance, in Latin America the unemployment decreased noticeably from 6.8% in 2014 to 5.8% in 2015. However, the level of unemployment went up from 7.5% in 2014 to 7.9% in 2015. Compared to the situation worldwide, South Asia was the only world region which level of unemployment was lower in both years given, namely 3.9% in 2014 and 2015.
