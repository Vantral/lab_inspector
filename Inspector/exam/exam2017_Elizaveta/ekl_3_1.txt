The diagramm gives a comparisson of the unemployment rate between 2014 and 2015 in a certain world regions. 
It is clear from the chart that the middle worldwide unemployment rate did not change. More than that, we can see that in 2 of 5 regions this rate is higher in 2014 than in 2015. 
As we can see, such regions, as Nothern Africa and South Asia have the same unemployment rate both in 2014 and 2015 (12,5% for N. Africa and 3,9% for S. Asia). Moreover, the total worldwide rate also did not change (5,9 per cent for both years). 
Speaking about changes, it is possible to admit that in the Middle East and in the Latin America the level of unemployment is higher in 2014 that in 2015 (11 per cent vs 9,6 per cent in the Middle East and 6,8 per cent vs 5,8 per cent in the Latin America). Also, we can admit that in the European Union the unemployement rate is higher on 0,4 per cent in 2015 than in 2014 (7,5 per cent in 2014 vs 7,9 per cent in 2015).
