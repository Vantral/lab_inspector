The line graph illustrates the difference in percents in earnings between men and women in Japan, USA, UK, Germany and New Zealand between 1980 and 1995. 
The common trend for all of theese countries is that to 1995 the percentage of diffrence in male and female earnings became less, then it was in 1980. Also, the beggest percentage for the whole period is related to Japan, while the smallest is related to Germany. 
It is clearly seem that from the 1980 to 1985 in Japan the percentage was slightly rising from 40% to 41%, but from the 1985 to 1995 it dramatically dicreased to 30%. In the USA for the whole period the percentage was minimalizing to only 20% from almost 40%. In the UK was the same trend, but it started with more than 35% in 1980 and fell to a little bit more then 20%. 
In Germany from 1980 to 1990 the percentage fell from 22,5% to 10%, but from 1990 to 1995 it rised to 12%. In New Zealand for the whole period the persantage dicreased from 20% to 11%.
