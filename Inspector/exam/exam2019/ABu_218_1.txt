The pie charts reflects the number of different age people groups who study various courses in a UK college in 2012. According to these free charts, total number of students attending science, art and sport and health courses is pretty similar: it is 650 people for science, 600 students for art and 630 people in total for last one. Young people from 18 to 25 years prefer to choose science because the percentage of such students involved in science is 55% which is the largest percentage for all the graphs. To compare, only 15% of people attending sports and health are considered to be a group of students from 18 to 25 years old. For the next age group which is 26-40 years old it is possible to notice that the majority of this age group have picked sports and health courses. 62% of 26-40 year-old students are invloved in this type of courses which is significantly more than the number of younger students. The portion of people in this age in science and arts is approximately equal: 30% of students from 26 to 40 years for science and 25% for arts. Finally, the age group from 40 years old and above varies from 15% of people involved in science to 45% in arts which is the highest percentage for this kind of courses. Overall, the number of different age people in a UK college in different courses significantly fluctuates.
