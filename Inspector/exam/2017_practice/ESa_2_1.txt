The charts illustrate the percentage of goods transported by rail and by road between Eastern European countries in 2008. Metals, food products, non-metallic minerals, chemicals, manufactured and machinery are considered in these charts. 
Overall, about quarter of good transported both by rail and by road is represented by manufactured goods. 35% of products that are transported by rail are metals, but metals represent only 11% of goods transported by road. Food products consist the largest percentage of goods transported by road, which is equal to 30%. Chemicals represent nearly the same part of goods trasported either by rail or by road, which is equal to 11% and 16% respectively. Machinery is mostly transported by rail, as it represents 12% of all of the goods transported this way and only 2% of goods transported by road. All in all, the percentage of each type of goods is different in respect to ways of transporting.
