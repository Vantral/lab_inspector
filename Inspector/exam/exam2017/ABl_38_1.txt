The chart represents the information about how the unemployment was changing in some world regions in 2014 and 2015. 
To begin with, the chart shows some common trends. For example, there are two regions where the unemployment rate remains the same in both years, these regions are the North Africa and the South Asia. Also, worldwide rate does not show any change in numbers. 
Nevertheless, some columns illustrate anouther trend. Both Middle East and Latin America has the decline of the unemployment rate in 2015. The case of the EU is different from all other regions. The unemployment rate in the UE increases in 2015. However, this region shows a comparatively little change in numbers. In 2014 the unemployment rate in the EU was 7,5%, while in 2015 it was 7,9%. Middle East represents the most big change in numbers, from 11% to 9,6%. Additionally, the highest unemployment rate is 12,5% in North Africa. The South Asia shows the lowest unemployment rate wich is equal to 3,9%.
