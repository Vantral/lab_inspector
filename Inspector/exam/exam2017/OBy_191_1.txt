The illustrations demonstrate proportions of goods transported by rail and by road between Eastern Europe countries in 2008. 
Even though, percentages for same kinds of products vary depending on type of transportation, manufactured goods are rather popular with approximately a quarter of the whole products' traffic between countries in Eastern Europe. As for metals, it's moved by rail roughly 3 times more often than by road. On the opposite, amount of food products transported by road is about 3 times larger. 
Meanwhile, there are some types of goods which are less popular to move between countries. For example, chemicals transportation takes from 11 up to 16 per cent depending on the method of moving them abroad. Similar situation can be noticed for machinery. However, the difference of amounts here are exactly 6 times for various ways of transportation. 
All in all, some kinds of goods are transported abroad more often in general than others. Moreover, the method of moving them from one country of Eastern Europe to another depends on a specific type of a product.
