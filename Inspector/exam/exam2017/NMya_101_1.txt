The following pie-charts provide a comparison of different goods' transportation in Eastern Europe in 2008. It can be seen that there are used two ways: railways and roads. 
The upper chart shows that the most significant part of goods transported by rail is metal and it takes 35%. On the second place are manufactured goods, which are taken a quoter of railways. The volume of chemicals and the volume of food products are similar and equal to 11% each. Only 12 percent of railways are used for machinery transportation. 
The lowest pie-chart absolutely differs from the first one. 30% of roads are provided for food products. Manufactured goods have approximately the same volume with food and are lower on 2%. 16% of roads are busy by chemicals. Non-metallic minerals are transported by road more frequently than by rail. Metals take only 11%. And the most insignificant portion is machinery with 2%.
