The charts below illustrate the information about the transportation of goods between countries in Eastern Europe in 2008. 
Speaking about the main feature, manufactured goods are second in rail and road transporting with a result of 25% for rail and 28% for road. Metals take a biggest part in goods transported by rail. Its percentage equals 35%, but in road transporting, metals have only 11% of whole amount of goods. Food products are transported by road more than other types of goods (30%), but in railway transporting it has the same result as a metals in road transporting. Chemicals placed 5th in transport in by rail and 3rd in goods transported by road. As for machinery, it took 12% of goods transported by rail and only 2% in road transporting. Non-metallic minerals are more popular in transporting by road (13%) than in transporting by rail (only 6%).
