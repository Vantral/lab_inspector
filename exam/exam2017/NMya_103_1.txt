The pie-chart indicates the percentage of goods transported by rail and by road within Eastern Europe in 2008. It is obvious that proportions of manufactured goods, and chemicals are practically the same, while the amount of other goods differs greatly. 
In detail, the major part of things transported by rail is Metals with its 35% and manufactured goods with 25%. The greater amount of transactions made by road is 30% for food products and 28% for manufactured goods. 
In both groups the quantity of machinery, chemicals and non-metallic minerals transported is not more than one-third of the whole deals made, but it is seen that machinery is preferred to be transported by rail and non-metallic minerals are most likely to be transported by road. 
It is clear, that heavy goods like metals are transported by rail making 35% of all the deals, while food products transportation creates 30% of all made by road transportation.
