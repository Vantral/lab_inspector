The pie charts demonstrate the amount of goods in percentage, which was delivered to Eastern Europe by different transport in 2008. 
Overall, the goods were transported either by rail or by road. The share of metals and machinery, delivered by trains was significantly larger, than amount of these goods transported by road. The amount of other goods was supplied practically in equal shares. 
In all the issues, which were transported by trains, the transportation of metals made 35% of the whole share. This is three times larger in comparison with share of metals delivered by road. The amount of machinery transported by motor vehicle made only 2% out of 100, which is six times less than transportation of this good by railways. 
The share of manufactured goods, chemicals and non-metallic minerals did not change significantly, but the transportation of food products by road made 30%, while it made only 11% by rail.
