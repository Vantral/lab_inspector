The piecharts compares the percentage of goods transported by rail and road in East-European countries in 2008. Railways are mostly used for metals transportation and roads for food products. 
The first diagram gives information about products, which is delivered by rail. It is noticeable that the mean feature of the first graph is percentage of transported metals (35%). In contrast, road transportation has only 11% of metals delivery, but it provides 28% of manufactured goods transportation. It is close to 25% of goods produced at manufactures, but transported by rail. 
In conclusion, the main feature of two pie charts are that the most popular method of transportation metals is railways, whereas food products are delivered by autobahns. For manufactured goods two types of transportation are appropriate.
