The chart illustrates how the level of unemployment was changing from 2014 to 2015 in different areas around the glob. At first glance it is clear that there is not a dawnward trend in the reduction of unemployment rate in all regions. 
As it can be seen from the figure, the highest level of unemployment is located in North Africa. It accounts for 12,5% and remains stable in both 2014 and 2015. Similarly, there is a stability in the rate of unemployed people in South Asia and around the world with 3,9% and 5,9% respectively. Although, countries in the EU show a slight rise from 7.5% to 7.9% in the amount of jobless citizens. 
There are still regions illustrating reduction in the unemployment rate. It is clear that there was a sharp diminution in the Middle East over the one year period. Moreover, Latin America shows dicrease from 6,8% to 5,8% as well.
