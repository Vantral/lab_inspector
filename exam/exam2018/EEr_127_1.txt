The following graph shows the percentage difference in earnings between men and women in different countries between 1980 and 1995. During all of that time, the percentage difference between the income of men and women in UK and New Zealand (initially being 23% and 20% respectively) was significantly lower than in the rest of the countries, and Japan was having the highest percentage during all of this period, starting with 40% in 1980. Between 1980 and 1985, the percentage difference in earnings between men and women in Japan and New Zealand had risen, whereas in all other countries, it has dropped, with USA experiencing the largest decrease of almost ten percent. Then, between 1985 and 1990, all the percentages have dropped. However, between 1990 and 1995 the difference of income between men and women in the UK has risen a little, whereas all the other percentages have dropped even more. Overall, the difference between male and female income has dropped throughout the years in every country, each percentage decreasing by around 10%.
